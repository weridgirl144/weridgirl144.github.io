Our favourite recipes from the global South

Read time: five minutes

.

""Food from the Global South isn't just sustenance—it's a passport to history, culture, and the soul of the people who crafted these dishes. No other cuisine wraps you in warmth, flavor, and tradition quite like this." - Chef Amara Gupta !" - Chef Amara Gupta, 2014

1. Feijoada (Brazil) Summary: A rich and hearty black bean stew traditionally made with various cuts of pork, Feijoada is one of Brazil's most iconic dishes. It’s often served with rice, collard greens, and orange slices. The dish reflects Brazil’s blend of indigenous, African, and Portuguese influences.

2. Jollof Rice (West Africa) Summary: A beloved and versatile dish, Jollof Rice consists of rice cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce with onions, bell peppers, and a variety of spices. It’s popular across West Africa, especially in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal, with each country having its own twist on the recipe.

3.Biryani (South Asia) Summary: Biryani is a fragrant rice dish cooked with spices, meat (commonly chicken or lamb), and sometimes vegetables. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, it has deep Persian roots, and its numerous regional varieties make it a staple across South Asia.

4. Bobotie (South Africa) Summary: Bobotie is a savory South African dish made with spiced minced meat, typically beef or lamb, and topped with an egg-based custard. It is often served with rice and chutney, reflecting the country’s diverse culinary influences, including Dutch and Malay.

5. Piri Piri Chicken (Mozambique/Portugal) Summary: Piri Piri Chicken, originally from Mozambique and popularized by Portuguese explorers, is a dish of grilled chicken marinated with a spicy piri piri sauce made from African bird’s eye chilies, lemon, and garlic. It represents a fusion of African and Portuguese flavors.
