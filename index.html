Top recipes from the global South

Read time: 10 minutes

.

"I love kittens!" - Maria, 2024

1. Feijoada (Brazil) Summary: A rich and hearty black bean stew traditionally made with various cuts of pork, Feijoada is one of Brazil's most iconic dishes. It’s often served with rice, collard greens, and orange slices. The dish reflects Brazil’s blend of indigenous, African, and Portuguese influences. 2. Jollof Rice (West Africa) Summary: A beloved and versatile dish, Jollof Rice consists of rice cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce with onions, bell peppers, and a variety of spices. It’s popular across West Africa, especially in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal, with each country having its own twist on the recipe. 3. Pho (Vietnam) Summary: Pho is a Vietnamese noodle soup made from beef or chicken broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat. It’s often eaten for breakfast but enjoyed at any time of day. Pho has become one of Vietnam’s most globally recognized dishes due to its fragrant and savory flavors. 4. Biryani (South Asia) Summary: Biryani is a fragrant rice dish cooked with spices, meat (commonly chicken or lamb), and sometimes vegetables. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, it has deep Persian roots, and its numerous regional varieties make it a staple across South Asia. 5. Arepas (Venezuela/Colombia) Summary: Arepas are round, flat cornmeal cakes that are grilled, baked, or fried and stuffed with a variety of fillings such as cheese, meats, or beans. Found in both Venezuela and Colombia, they are a versatile and staple food for breakfast or any meal. 6. Bobotie (South Africa) Summary: Bobotie is a savory South African dish made with spiced minced meat, typically beef or lamb, and topped with an egg-based custard. It is often served with rice and chutney, reflecting the country’s diverse culinary influences, including Dutch and Malay. 7. Nasi Goreng (Indonesia) Summary: Nasi Goreng is a popular Indonesian fried rice dish flavored with sweet soy sauce, garlic, shallots, and chili, and often topped with a fried egg. It’s enjoyed across Southeast Asia and is often served with chicken, shrimp, or vegetables. 8. Tamales (Mexico) Summary: Tamales are a traditional Mesoamerican dish made of corn dough (masa) filled with meats, cheese, or vegetables, and wrapped in corn husks or banana leaves before being steamed. They have been a significant part of Mexican cuisine since pre-Columbian times. 9. Piri Piri Chicken (Mozambique/Portugal) Summary: Piri Piri Chicken, originally from Mozambique and popularized by Portuguese explorers, is a dish of grilled chicken marinated with a spicy piri piri sauce made from African bird’s eye chilies, lemon, and garlic. It represents a fusion of African and Portuguese flavors. 10. Laksa (Malaysia/Singapore) Summary: Laksa is a flavorful noodle soup with a spicy coconut or tamarind-based broth, typically made with prawns or chicken and served with rice noodles. It’s a hallmark of Southeast Asian cuisine, blending Malay, Chinese, and Indian culinary traditions.
